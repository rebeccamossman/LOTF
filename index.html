How Most People Start

Most cybersecurity professionals don't start in security — they build a foundation in general IT first, then specialize. There is no single right path.

General IT First

Help Desk Technician, Network Administrator, Systems Administrator, or Software Developer — these roles build the foundation security work depends on.

Entry-Level Security

Cybersecurity Analyst and Information Security Analyst are the most common first steps directly into the field.

Education

56% of professionals hold a bachelor's degree and 23% hold an associate degree — but neither is required to get started.

Self-Taught Paths

Certifications, bootcamps, and free platforms like TryHackMe and Hack The Box are legitimate paths. Hands-on practice often matters more than credentials alone.

The 5 Cybersecurity Career Paths

Cybersecurity isn't one job — it's a family of disciplines. Find the area that fits your strengths and interests.

Engineering & Architecture

Build and implement defense systems. Leads to Security Architect roles.

Certs: Security+, SSCP, CISSP · Avg: $128K–$164K
Incident Response & Forensics

Monitor networks, contain breaches, and investigate attacks after they happen.

Certs: GCIH, ECIH, CCE · Avg: $97K–$132K
Management & Leadership

Oversee teams, budgets, and strategy. Top role: CISO (Chief Information Security Officer).

Certs: CISM, GCPM, CISSP · Avg: $180K–$198K
Consulting & Pen Testing

Test systems for multiple clients — a mix of offensive and defensive work.

Certs: Security+, OSCP, CSC · Avg: ~$119K
Governance, Risk & Compliance

Ensure organizations meet legal and regulatory requirements. Great for detail-oriented, policy-minded people.

Certs: CISM, CRISC, CISSP

How to Get Started

You can start building real cybersecurity skills today — most of the best resources are free. Always get a parent or guardian's permission before signing up for any online platform.

🧪 Learn by Doing

Hack The Box, TryHackMe, pwn.college, and OverTheWire offer free guided labs that teach real skills hands-on — no experience required to start.

📜 Get Certified

CompTIA Security+ is the most recognized entry-level cert. CEH and OSCP are intermediate. CISSP is for experienced professionals.

🤝 Join the Community

DC702 is Las Vegas's own DEF CON group. C2Society hosts local workshops. DEF CON — the world's largest hacker conference — is held right here every year.

🚩 Compete in CTFs

Capture the Flag competitions are puzzle-based hacking contests. A great way to learn, meet people, and build a portfolio that employers actually notice.

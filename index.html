Why Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is one of the fastest-growing, highest-paying, and most future-proof fields you can enter — and it genuinely matters. The work protects hospitals, schools, governments, and millions of people every day.

  • 33% job growth projected 2023–2033 — far above the national average. (Bureau of Labor Statistics)
  • Jobs outnumber qualified candidates — demand consistently outpaces supply across every specialization.
  • $128K average salary for security engineers. (Glassdoor, 2025)
  • Diverse opportunities — from offensive hacking to policy, forensics, engineering, and leadership.
  • Dynamic work — the threat landscape changes constantly, so no two days are the same.

How Most People Start

Most cybersecurity professionals don't start in security — they build a foundation in general IT first, then specialize. There is no single right path.

  • General IT first — Help Desk Technician, Network Administrator, Systems Administrator, or Software Developer. These roles build the foundational knowledge security work depends on.
  • Entry-level security roles — Cybersecurity Analyst and Information Security Analyst are common first steps into the field.
  • Education — 56% of professionals hold a bachelor's degree; 23% hold an associate degree.
  • Self-taught paths are valid — Certifications, bootcamps, and online programs (TryHackMe, Hack The Box) accelerate the path. Hands-on practice often matters more than credentials alone.

The 5 Cybersecurity Career Paths

Cybersecurity isn't one job — it's a family of disciplines. Find the area that fits your strengths and interests:

  • Engineering & Architecture — Build and implement defense systems. Leads to Security Architect roles. Certs: Security+, SSCP, CISSP. Avg: $128K–$164K.
  • Incident Response & Forensics — Monitor networks, contain breaches, investigate attacks. Certs: GCIH, ECIH, CCE. Avg: $97K–$132K.
  • Management & GRC — Oversee teams, risk, and compliance. Top role: CISO (Chief Information Security Officer). Certs: CISM, GCPM, CISSP.
  • Consulting & Penetration Testing — Test systems for multiple clients, offense and defense combined. Certs: Security+, OSCP, CSC. Avg: ~$119K; senior roles $180K–$198K.
  • Governance, Risk & Compliance (GRC) — Ensure organizations meet legal and regulatory security requirements. Great path for detail-oriented and policy-minded people.

How to Get Started

You can start building real cybersecurity skills today — most of the best resources are free. Always get a parent or guardian's permission before signing up for any online platform.

  • Learn by doing — Hack The Box, TryHackMe, pwn.college, and OverTheWire offer free guided labs and challenges that teach real skills hands-on.
  • Get certified — CompTIA Security+ is the most recognized entry-level cert. CEH and OSCP are intermediate. CISSP is advanced.
  • Join the community — DC702 is Las Vegas's own DEF CON group. C2Society hosts local workshops. DEF CON itself is held here every year.
  • Compete in CTFs — Capture the Flag competitions are puzzle-based hacking contests. They're a great way to learn, meet people, and build a portfolio.

Key Takeaways

  • Cybersecurity has diverse paths — find the one that fits your personality and strengths.
  • 33% job growth through 2033. Demand consistently outpaces the supply of qualified candidates.
  • Ethical hacking requires explicit authorization — always. The law applies to everyone.
  • Your digital footprint is much larger than you think — protecting yourself starts with awareness.
  • Las Vegas has a world-class security community. DC702, C2Society, and DEF CON are right here.
